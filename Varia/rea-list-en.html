Simple rule

You are planning to fuse two glasses together. Check the lists on this page.
1. Of one of the colors is not in the lists, no reaction
2. If both appear in the lists but only one is marked S , better test with a sample piece if the reaction disturbs your design
3. if both are marked S no reaction

 
Between blue and orange a strong reaction is seen. Between orange and green is a weaker reaction. Transparent blue overlapping yellow looks green. Under it is a reaction layer. The broas line over the blue, is only a shadow .

Sulphur (S) is used in some glass colours. When fused to another glass that contains oxides (Cu, Pb) brown or grey sulphide stripe is formed in the contact surface. If the pieces are fused on top of another, the large contact area is coloured.

If needed, the contact surfaces can be isolates with clear glass powder and glue.
